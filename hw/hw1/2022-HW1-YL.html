Question 3 (2 points)¶

Let's stay with the graph of Question 2 for a moment longer.

Consider a hypothetical spreading process on the network. We'll talk a lot more about spreading processes later in class, but one of the main ideas is that any spreading processes can only move along the edges of the graph. This means that if there is no path from node A to node B, the process cannot get from A to B or from B to A. We will assume in this question that if one node in a component gets infected, all nodes in the component will eventually get infected.

(a) One of the nodes in this graph, chosen uniformly at random, becomes infected. What is the probability that eventually more than half of the nodes in the graph become infected?

(b) One of the nodes in this graph, chosen uniformly at random, becomes infected. What is the probability that eventually at most 10% of all nodes become infected?
